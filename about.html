Brief Explanation of our Prototype

The primary issue we identified is “Slow Internet Connection.” This problem frequently occurs in schools due to multiple users accessing the network at the same time, improper router placement, and limited bandwidth.

Slow connectivity can negatively impact online classes, research activities, digital submissions, and communication between teachers and students. Recognizing this challenge, our group developed a Digital Poster as an awareness-based solution.

Why We Chose a Digital Poster

As Grade 8 students, we acknowledge that purchasing additional routers or upgrading internet infrastructure may not be practical or financially possible. Instead of proposing expensive solutions, we focused on an informative approach that promotes proper internet usage and strategic router placement.

Our digital poster aims to educate students about responsible internet behavior, minimizing unnecessary downloads, limiting background applications, and understanding how network congestion affects performance.

Objectives of the Project

• To raise awareness about the causes of slow internet connection in schools.
• To suggest practical and achievable solutions.
• To encourage responsible internet usage among students.
• To demonstrate how digital media can be used to address real-world problems.
